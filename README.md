## PROCEDURE: 
 Given Data:
 ### Angular velocity of crank AB = 36 rad/s


### Length AB = 200 mm = 0.2 m


### Length BC = 400 mm = 0.4 m


### Length AD = 600 mm = 0.6 m (fixed link)


### At the given instant AB is perpendicular to AD


### Find:


### Velocity and acceleration of midpoint of BC


### Velocity and acceleration of a point on CD located 100 mm from D



               

### 1. Draw the Space Diagram:

### First draw the configuration of the mechanism.
### Draw the fixed link AD = 600 mm horizontally.
### From point A, draw AB = 200 mm such that AB is perpendicular to AD.
### From point B, draw BC = 400 mm.
### Join C to D to complete the four-bar mechanism ABCD.
### Mark the midpoint M of BC.
### Mark point P on CD, which is 100 mm from D.
### This diagram represents the actual position of the mechanism at that instant.

### 2. Velocity Analysis:
### Step 1: Velocity of Point B
### The crank AB rotates with angular velocity 36 rad/s.
### The linear velocity of point B is calculated by:
### Substituting values:
### VB​=ωAB​×AB
### VB​=36×0.2 
### VB​=7.2m/s

### The direction of velocity of B is perpendicular to AB.
### Step 2: Draw the Velocity Diagram
### Choose a point o as the origin of the velocity diagram.
### Draw vector ob = 7.2 m/s perpendicular to AB.
### From b, draw a line perpendicular to BC.
### (This represents the relative velocity of C with respect to B.)
### From o, draw a line perpendicular to CD.
### The intersection of these two lines gives point c.
### Now:
### oc represents velocity of point C
### bc represents velocity of C relative to B

### Step 3: Velocity of Midpoint M on BC
### In the space diagram, M is the midpoint of BC.
### In the velocity diagram, locate point m midway between b and c.
### Measure om.
### The velocity of midpoint M is:
###                                               VM​=(Velocity scale)×om

### Step 4: Velocity of Point P on CD
### Point P lies 100 mm from D on link CD.
### Steps:
### Measure length DC in the space diagram.
### Determine the ratio:
 ###                            DC/DP 
### In the velocity diagram, divide oc in the same ratio to locate point p.
### Measure op.
### Velocity of point P is:
  ###                                VP​=(Velocity scale)×op

### 3. Acceleration Analysis
### Step 1: Acceleration of Point B
### Acceleration of point B consists of:
### Radial acceleration
### Tangential acceleration
### Since the crank rotates at constant angular velocity, tangential acceleration is zero.
### Radial acceleration is given by:
###                                             aB​=ω2×AB
### Substitute values:
 ###                             aB​=(36*36)×0.2
 ###                             aB=259.2 m/s**2
### Direction: from B towards A.

### Step 2: Draw Acceleration Diagram
### Take a point o₁ as the origin of acceleration diagram.
### Draw vector o₁b₁ = 259.2 m/s² towards A.
### From b₁, draw:
### A line parallel to BC (radial acceleration component).
###A line perpendicular to BC (tangential acceleration component).
### From o₁, draw:
### A line parallel to CD
### A line perpendicular to CD
### The intersection of these lines gives point c₁, representing acceleration of point C.

### Step 3: Acceleration of Midpoint M
### In the acceleration diagram, locate m₁ midway between b₁ and c₁.
### Measure o₁m₁.
### Acceleration of midpoint M:
###                                             aM​=(Acceleration scale)×o1​m1​
                                        
### Step 4: Acceleration of Point P on CD
### In the acceleration diagram, divide c₁d₁ according to the ratio DP/DC.
### Locate point p₁.
### Measure o₁p₁.
### Acceleration of point P:
###                                     aP​=(Acceleration scale)×o1​p1
